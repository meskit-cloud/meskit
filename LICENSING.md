MESkit is distributed under the Business Source License 1.1 in
[LICENSE](LICENSE).

This means:

- You may read the source code, modify it, and self-host it for your own
  internal business use.
- A manufacturer may run MESkit in production for its own factory operations.
- Employees and contractors may deploy or operate MESkit on behalf of that
  manufacturer for the manufacturer's internal use.
- You may not sell MESkit, offer it as a hosted service, white-label it, bundle
  it into a commercial product for third parties, or resell access to it
  without a separate written commercial agreement.

## Plain-English Rules

Allowed without a separate agreement:

- A manufacturer self-hosts MESkit for its own factory.
- A company modifies MESkit and uses it internally.
- A contractor installs or customizes MESkit for one customer's internal use,
  as long as the customer is not being sold MESkit as a hosted or resale
  product.

Not allowed without a separate agreement:

- Offering "MESkit Cloud" or a similar hosted service to customers.
- Reselling MESkit access to multiple customers.
- White-labeling MESkit as your own MES product.
- Embedding MESkit in a paid third-party software offering where MESkit is a
  material part of the value being sold.

## Important Terminology

This license is source-available, not OSI open source.

If you want to describe MESkit accurately in public copy, use language like:

- "Source-available"
- "Free to self-host for internal use"
- "Commercial license required for hosted or resale use"

Avoid saying only "MIT" or "open source" unless you change the license again.
